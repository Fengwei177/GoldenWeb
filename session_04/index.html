Miao Ying

Miao Ying was born in Shanghai, China. She studied at Chinese Academy of Fine arts and Alfred University.

Miao's art focuses on Chinese internet culture and censorship, as well as how contemporary technology

influence our daily lives. Many of her works use parodies and humor to give viewers a glimpse of what the

internet is like inside the great fire wall, and how unique culture and memes develop under government

censorship and self-censorship.


Miao's Blind Spot (2007) is a manually annotated Mandarin dictionary. These annotation are the 2000 censored

search terms on google.cn at that time. Miao spent three month searching all words in the dictionary from A to

Z, finding the 2000 censored terms and erasing them in the dictionary using white tape. The work comments on

heavy censorship on Chinese internet and the human labor to enforce such strict regulations.

Victoria Vesna

Victoria Vesna was born in Washington, D.C. United States. She is a professor at the UCLA Department of Design

Media Arts. Vesna is not only interested in web art, but her works also collaborate with nano-science, neuroscience,

and biology. She works across different disciplines and works with different medias. She does creative experimental

research, and is particularly interested in how technological innovation affects identity and percepton.


Vesna's Body© Incorporated is a website that allows users to create their own avatars. Some avatars were displayed at

installations of this work. Users can customize their avatars' gender, age, size, and material of the 3D model. But

before creating the avatars, users must agree to a series of terms and conditions, some borrowed from Disney's wedsite,

giving up their rights and ownership to their avatars. Body© Incorporated comments on internet indentity and ownship,

the works reflects many social media and online services' control our online indentity, private images and private infos.
